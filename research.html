Research projects (updating)

The spectral transition data of high-temperature air plasma are important physical properties for the design of thermal protection system, target identification and tracking, penetration and stealth. At present, the spectral transition data of air plasma at high temperatures is very scarce, which has become a bottleneck limiting the analysis and study of thermal radiation of high-temperature ai plasma. This project aims at the frontier research in thermal radiation, studies the key problems in the spectral modelling of high-temperature air plasma. Various quantum chemistry methods are used to study the electronic structures and correction effects (including core-valence, scalar relativistic and spin-orbit corrections) of the components for the high-temperature air plasma. The mechanisms that affect electronic structures by various corrections are analyzed to establish the computational models of first principles, which can accurately describe the transitions between high states. Available experimental data in the literature is used to refine the electronic structures, so as for accurate spectral modelling of high-temperature air plasma. The complete and accurate spectral transition data is constructed to provide the basic radiative properties for the research of thermal radiation from high-temperature air plasma.
